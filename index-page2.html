Exchange rules

This offer contains the main terms, conditions and rules, based on which the Sowa service provides services to its customers. Before using the services of this service, the user must agree to all of the following conditions. Only by accepting all conditions without exception, the user can proceed to use the service. These exchange rules are posted on the Sowa resource in the public domain

Exchange rules

  • 1.Key definitions, concepts and terms

  • 1.1The Sowa service (next is the Service) is a service that provides services to individuals and legal entities in terms of exchanging, buying or selling electronic currency. These services are provided through a special software package

  • 1.2User is a legal / natural person who has unconditionally accepted all the terms of this offer and uses the services of the sowa

  • 1.3Electronic money (electronic currency) is monetary or other obligations that arise between persons using this currency and those who created it. The mentioned obligations are in digital form

  • 1.4Operations (payments) is the transfer of digital currency (electronic money) from the sender's account to the recipient's account

  • 1.5Application - a specific intention of the visitor of the sova.gg resource to use its services by accepting the relevant rules of this offer. This intention is expressed in the form of providing information by the user and its transfer to the service through a special electronic form, which is located on the resource sowa

  • 1.6The original electronic currency is the currency that the user has and which he wants to exchange or sell, receiving in return other electronic currency or money in a different form

  • 1.7The received currency is the currency that the user of the sowa service receives as a result of the exchange, transferring the original currency of the service

  • 1.8Parties are all users who unconditionally accepted the rules specified in this agreement and decided to use the services of the sowa service, as well as the sowa service itself

  • 1.9Currency exchange is a transaction for the exchange of electronic currency of various payment systems

  • 1.10Currency reserves are the amount of electronic money that the sova.gg service has in its possession, and within which users can carry out exchange transactions through this service. Each user can get up-to-date information about the amount of reserves by visiting the ''reserves'' page of this service

  • 1.11The rate is the ratio of one electronic currency in relation to another in the process of making an exchange

  • 2Subject of the agreement. The order when the agreement comes into force

  • 2.1

    The subject of this agreement is the provision of a number of services to sowa usersnamely:

  • 2.1.1Services for the exchange of one electronic currency for another or for money in a different form

  • 2.1.2ervices for the sale of electronic money

  • 2.1.3Services for the acquisition of electronic money

  • 2.2Acceptance of the terms of this agreement by the user is carried out when:

  • 2.2.1Filling out the application form for the exchange and its further direction for execution to the service

  • 2.3When forming an application for an exchange, the user confirms his agreement with all the rules of the exchange and confirms his intention to use the services of the service on the conditions offered to him

  • 2.3.1This agreement comes into force as soon as the details of the sowa service receive funds from the user for further crediting him with electronic currency or money in the amount provided for by the terms of the application

  • 2.3.2As soon as the funds are received on the details specified by the user, the current agreement automatically loses its validity. Also, this action occurs when the user exchange request is cancelled

  • 2.4By accepting this agreement in electronic form, each of the parties thereby recognizes its legal force, equivalent to a similar agreement in writing

  • 2.5Any changes to the current agreement can be made by the sowa service without the need to notify the user of such changes. However, this does not cancel the obligation of the service to post the current version of the agreement on the sowa website

  • 3.Cost of services. Subject of the agreement

  • 3.1The subject of the current agreement are the services of the service in terms of the sale, purchase or exchange of electronic currency of various payment systems. All of the above operations can be carried out solely on the initiative of the user and only if he agrees with all the terms of the agreement

  • 3.2For the services provided to the user, the latter pays the service a commission, the amount of which is displayed in the exchange request. This remuneration is paid by the user only after he agrees to the exchange

  • 3.3The amount of the commission fee of the sowa service for the above actions is reflected in the application in the form of an exchange rate and is confirmed by the user on one of the pages of the user interface

  • 4.Privacy Policy

  • 4.1In order to carry out the operation of exchanging, buying or selling electronic money or cryptocurrency, the service asks the user for personal data, which it undertakes to store in encrypted form and not transfer to third parties, in addition to the situations mentioned in clauses 4.4, 4.5 of this agreement.

  • 4.2Transactions based on requests received from clients, as well as the transfer of client data from them to the service, are carried out via a special encrypted SSL channel (key length is 256 bits)

  • 4.3If this becomes necessary, the service reserves the right, on its own initiative, to collect additional information about the user, by any means that do not contradict the law. One way or another, but any information collected about the client will not be transferred to third parties. Exceptions are clauses 4.4, 4.5 of this agreement

  • 4.4The transfer of personal data of clients, as well as details of transactions carried out by them, can be transferred by a court decision, an official request received in writing from law enforcement agencies, as well as on the service’s own initiative. At the same time, the confidential status of such data will be preserved

  • 4.5The transfer of details of a transaction made by the user may be transferred by the sowa service at the official request of the Electronic Settlement System, if the purpose of such request is to conduct an internal investigation

  • 4.6Any data that the service receives from users is further stored in its database for3 years after the last transaction was made by the user. The same is true for the details of the operations they carried out

  • 5.Policy for combating illegal transactions and money laundering

  • 5.1According to the current international legislation, the sova.gg service follows a certain list of rules and performs procedures aimed at preventing the use of sova for transactions related to money laundering, that is, those transactions whose main purpose is to return illegally obtained securities or money to financial and economic market, to further provide and possession of legal methods. Also, the service stops all transactions that are illegal

  • 5.2In order to successfully prevent illegal transactions, the service has adopted some requirements for requests submitted by customers:

  • 5.2.1The recipient and sender of the payment on the application must not be different persons. Transfers to third parties are prohibited

  • 5.2.2All personal and contact data that the client of the service indicates in the application must be up-to-date at the time of filing such an application. The reliability of such data should not be questioned

  • 5.2.3Tickets created by users using anonymous connections will not be processed by the service. The creation of such applications is prohibited by sowa

  • 5.3 In order to stop possible illegal operations, sowa:

    — Limits user transactions. The limits set depend on factors such as the user's country of residence, degree of identification, etc.

    — Applies its own system of auto-analysis of the client's actions and their transactions. At the same time, all transactions that seem suspicious to the service will be urgently stopped.

    — Always adheres to the generally accepted policy that says - “know who your client is”.

    — Mandatory checks the information received from users by all available means.
